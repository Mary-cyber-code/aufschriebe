Language Comparison: English, German & Japanese

1. The Writing Systems

English

Uses the standard Latin alphabet with 26 letters. No accents or special characters.

German

Uses the Latin alphabet, but includes 4 special characters: umlauts (ä, ö, ü) and "Eszett" (ß).

Japanese

Combines three writing systems:

  • Hiragana: Grammar/native words.
  • Katakana: Foreign names/loanwords.
  • Kanji: Complex ideas from China.

2. Vocabulary & Sentences

Basic Vocabulary

  • Hello: Hallo / Hello / こんにちは (Konnichiwa)
  • Thank you: Danke / Thank you / ありがとう (Arigatou)
  • Apple: Apfel / Apple / りんご (Ringo)
  • Water: Wasser / Water / 水 (Mizu)

Example Sentences

"I love you."
DE: Ich liebe dich.
JP: 愛しています (Aishite imasu.)

"I am learning English."
DE: Ich lerne Englisch.
JP: 私は英語を学んでいます (Watashi wa eigo o manande imasu.)

3. Sentence Structure (Word Order)

English & German (S-V-O)

They usually follow the Subject-Verb-Object order.

I (Subject) eat (Verb) an apple (Object).

Japanese (S-O-V)

Always puts the verb at the very end: Subject-Object-Verb.

I (Subject) an apple (Object) eat (Verb).

4. Articles & Nouns

German

Very complex. Three genders and many articles depending on cases (der, die, das...).

English

Very simple. Mostly uses just one definite article ("the") and "a/an".

Japanese

Super easy. No articles and no plural forms.
